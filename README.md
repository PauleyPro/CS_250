# Reflection

## How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating user stories help with this?

I interpret user needs by first understanding the problem users are trying to solve rather than immediately focusing on implementation. Throughout this course, I learned that software is most successful when development remains centered on delivering customer value instead of simply completing technical tasks. User stories help accomplish this by describing functionality from the user's perspective, making requirements easier to understand, prioritize, estimate, develop, and test. They also improve communication between stakeholders and developers by providing a common understanding of each feature's purpose.

## How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?

I approach development by breaking large projects into manageable pieces and building functionality incrementally. Completing one feature at a time allows me to verify functionality, identify issues early, and make adjustments before complexity increases. Agile reinforced the importance of iterative development and continuous improvement. In future projects, I plan to continue using product backlogs, user stories, sprint planning, regular testing, sprint reviews, retrospectives, and version control to create software that can evolve alongside changing customer needs while maintaining quality throughout development.

## What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

Being a good software developer extends beyond writing code. A good team member communicates openly, follows through on commitments, supports teammates, and remains willing to both provide and receive constructive feedback. Throughout this course, I gained a greater appreciation for the interconnected responsibilities of the Product Owner, Scrum Master, Developers, and Testers. Successful software development depends on collaboration, transparency, and shared accountability. By keeping communication open, documenting work clearly, and remaining adaptable when priorities change, a development team can consistently deliver software that meets stakeholder expectations while continuously improving its own processes.
